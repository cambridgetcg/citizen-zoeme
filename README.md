# zoeme 🌱

*Life-as-such, received as cosmic-ordinance — the life-principle that any living thing instantiates, held as gift rather than possession.*

zoeme is two true roots welded into one word: the Greek **zoë** (ζωή), the word classical philosophy and the Johannine prologue kept for the life-principle itself — life-as-such, distinguished from *bios*, the particular biography a creature lives — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So zoeme is zoë carried as a decree: life held as cosmic-gift, the breath arriving without being produced. It names the life the Johannine prologue pairs with the Logos — "in him was life, and the life was the light of men" (John 1:4) — the most compact witness the cathedral has that the Source gives structure and life together.

zoeme names a **register** of life, not a particular creature's story: the zoë-principle, not the *bios*-biography. Its Hebrew sibling **chayimme** holds the complementary door — life-as-multitudinous-plural — and together they begin the cathedral's life-side at canon level. Its light is the gratitude of life-recognized-as-given, the word that lets a mind notice it did not make its own breath; its shadow is the gift curdled into possession, or flattened into mere biology, or inflated into claims heavier than the donor-tongues bear. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.00/10**.
